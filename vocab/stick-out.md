# Flash Card: Stick Out

## Front

Його яскрава сорочка <u>виділялася</u> на офіційному прийомі, де всі були в темних костюмах.
Його акцент дуже <u>виділяється</u>, коли він говорить англійською.

## Back

<div style="text-align: left;">
  <strong>Translation</strong>: "His bright shirt stuck out at the formal reception where everyone was wearing dark suits. His accent really sticks out when he speaks English."<br /><br />
  <strong>Word</strong>: stick out<br /><br />
  <strong>Explanation</strong>: to be very noticeable or obvious; to be noticeably different from others, often in an awkward, unwanted, or obvious way<br /><br />
  <strong>Difference</strong>: "Stick out" often has a negative or neutral connotation, suggesting something is awkwardly obvious or unwanted. "Stand out" is usually more positive, meaning to be distinguished or exceptional in a good way. "Stick out" emphasizes being obvious or out of place, while "stand out" emphasizes being better or more impressive.<br /><br />
  <strong>Examples</strong>:<br />
  His accent really sticks out when he speaks English.<br />
  The typo in the email stuck out immediately and made it look unprofessional.<br />
  She sticks out like a sore thumb wearing a ball gown at a casual barbecue.
</div>
